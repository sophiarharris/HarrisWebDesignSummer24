My Favorite Animal: Elephant

My favorite animal is an elephant because they are so beautiful in so many ways. Not only are they physically majestic, but elephants also have lots of rich symbolism from various cultures around the world. Continue reading to learn more about these attributes, and by the end I'm sure you'll love elephants as much as I do!

Scientific Quick Facts

  • Elephants are the largest land mammals in the world
  • There are three elephant species: African Savanna, also known as "Bush", African Forest, and Asian
  • Baby elephants learn to walk within one hour of being born
  • The elephant population has declined by fifty percent in the past three generations, so the World Wide Fund for Nature has stepped in to help. Learn more about their efforts here: How WWF is Helping African Elephants
  • A wild elephant can consume up to six-hundred pounds of food per day
  • An elephant's trunk has over 150,000 muscle units
  • Elephants are the only land mammals that do not have the ability to jump
  • Elephants can swim by using their trunk as a snorkel

Cultural Symbolism

  • African symbol of strength
  • Indian symbol of royalty
  • Korean symbol of intelligence
  • Thai symbol of long life
  • Muslim symbol of the year the prophet Muhammad was born
